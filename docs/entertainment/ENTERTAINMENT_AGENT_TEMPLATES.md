# Entertainment Agent - Movie and TV Show Templates

**BULLY!** Roosevelt's Entertainment Intelligence Agent templates for organizing your movie and TV show library!

## Overview

The Entertainment Agent uses markdown files to store information about movies, TV shows, and episodes. These files should be:

- **Tagged appropriately** with `movie`, `tv_show`, or `tv_episode` tags
- **Categorized** as `entertainment` for easy filtering
- **Rich with metadata** (cast, directors, genres, ratings, plot summaries)
- **Vectorized** through the standard document processing pipeline

## Naming Conventions

### Movies
Format: `{Movie Title} ({Year}).md`

Examples:
- `The Godfather (1972).md`
- `Inception (2010).md`
- `Schindlers List (1993).md` (avoid special characters in filenames)

### TV Series (Overall)
Format: `{Series Title}.md`

Examples:
- `Breaking Bad.md`
- `The Wire.md`
- `Game of Thrones.md`

### TV Episodes
Format: `{Series Title} - S{Season}E{Episode}.md`

Examples:
- `Breaking Bad - S01E01.md`
- `Game of Thrones - S01E01.md`
- `The Wire - S03E11.md`

```markdown
# The Godfather (1972)

**Director**: Francis Ford Coppola  
**Writers**: Mario Puzo, Francis Ford Coppola  
**Stars**: Marlon Brando, Al Pacino, James Caan  
**Genre**: Crime, Drama  
**Runtime**: 175 minutes  
**Rating**: 9.2/10 (IMDb)  
**Release Date**: March 24, 1972  
**Production Companies**: Paramount Pictures, Alfran Productions  
**Budget**: $6-7 million  
**Box Office**: $250-291 million

## Plot Summary

The aging patriarch of an organized crime dynasty transfers control of his clandestine empire to his reluctant son. Vito Corleone is the head of a crime family in New York. When his youngest son Michael returns from World War II, he finds himself drawn into the family business despite his attempts to distance himself from it.

## Main Cast

- **Marlon Brando** as Vito Corleone - The aging patriarch of the Corleone crime family
- **Al Pacino** as Michael Corleone - Vito's youngest son who becomes involved in the family business
- **James Caan** as Sonny Corleone - Vito's hot-headed eldest son
- **Robert Duvall** as Tom Hagen - The family's consigliere (advisor)
- **Diane Keaton** as Kay Adams - Michael's girlfriend

## Themes

- **Family loyalty** - The bonds of family override all other considerations
- **Power and corruption** - The corrupting influence of power and the moral compromises required to maintain it
- **The American Dream** - The immigrant experience and the dark side of success in America
- **Transformation** - Michael's evolution from war hero to ruthless mob boss
- **Tradition vs modernity** - Old-world values clashing with new American ways

## Critical Reception

- Won 3 Academy Awards: Best Picture, Best Actor (Brando), Best Adapted Screenplay
- Nominated for 11 Oscars total
- Considered one of the greatest films ever made
- Ranked #2 on AFI's 100 Years...100 Movies list
- Preservation in the U.S. National Film Registry

## Trivia

- Marlon Brando won Best Actor but refused the award in protest of Hollywood's treatment of Native Americans
- The cat in the opening scene was a stray found on the Paramount lot
- The famous line "I'm gonna make him an offer he can't refuse" was ranked #2 on AFI's 100 Years...100 Quotes
- Real-life mobsters were consulted for authenticity
- The horse head scene used a real horse head from a dog food factory

## Memorable Quotes

- "I'm gonna make him an offer he can't refuse."
- "Leave the gun. Take the cannoli."
- "It's not personal, Sonny. It's strictly business."
- "A man who doesn't spend time with his family can never be a real man."

## Sequels

- The Godfather Part II (1974) - Widely considered one of the greatest sequels ever made
- The Godfather Part III (1990) - Completed the trilogy

## Related Movies

If you enjoyed The Godfather, you might also like:
- Goodfellas (1990)
- The Departed (2006)
- Casino (1995)
- Scarface (1983)
- Once Upon a Time in America (1984)
```

```markdown
# Breaking Bad

**Creator**: Vince Gilligan  
**Stars**: Bryan Cranston, Aaron Paul, Anna Gunn  
**Genre**: Crime, Drama, Thriller  
**Seasons**: 5  
**Total Episodes**: 62  
**Original Network**: AMC  
**Rating**: 9.5/10 (IMDb)  
**Run**: 2008-2013  
**Production Company**: High Bridge Entertainment, Gran Via Productions, Sony Pictures Television

## Overview

Breaking Bad follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. Facing financial ruin and a grim future for his family, he partners with former student Jesse Pinkman to cook and sell crystal methamphetamine. What begins as a desperate bid to secure his family's future transforms into a descent into the criminal underworld.

## Main Cast

- **Bryan Cranston** as Walter White / Heisenberg - High school chemistry teacher turned meth kingpin
- **Aaron Paul** as Jesse Pinkman - Small-time meth cook and Walter's partner
- **Anna Gunn** as Skyler White - Walter's wife
- **Dean Norris** as Hank Schrader - Walter's DEA agent brother-in-law
- **Betsy Brandt** as Marie Schrader - Skyler's sister
- **RJ Mitte** as Walter White Jr. - Walter and Skyler's son
- **Bob Odenkirk** as Saul Goodman - Criminal lawyer (spin-off: Better Call Saul)

## Themes

- **Moral decay** - Walter's transformation from mild-mannered teacher to ruthless criminal
- **Consequences** - Every action has repercussions, often tragic
- **Family** - The impact of Walter's choices on his loved ones
- **Identity** - Walter's dual identity as teacher and drug lord
- **Pride and ego** - Walter's pride drives many of his worst decisions
- **Power** - The intoxication and corruption of power

## Season Breakdown

### Season 1 (2008) - 7 episodes
Walter's cancer diagnosis and entry into the meth business. Introduction of core characters and initial conflicts.

### Season 2 (2009) - 13 episodes
Expansion of operations, conflict with drug dealer Tuco, and the aftermath of Jane's death.

### Season 3 (2010) - 13 episodes
Walter and Gus Fring's partnership begins. Hank's investigation intensifies.

### Season 4 (2011) - 13 episodes
Walter vs. Gus escalates to life-or-death stakes. The explosive finale "Face Off."

### Season 5 (2012-2013) - 16 episodes
The final season split into two parts. Walter's empire rises and falls. The legendary conclusion.
```

## TV Episode Template

```markdown
# Breaking Bad - S01E01 - Pilot

**Episode Title**: Pilot  
**Season**: 1  
**Episode Number**: 1  
**Air Date**: January 20, 2008  
**Director**: Vince Gilligan  
**Writer**: Vince Gilligan  
**Runtime**: 58 minutes  
**Rating**: 9.0/10 (IMDb)

## Episode Synopsis

High school chemistry teacher Walter White is diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer. Desperate to secure his family's financial future before his death, he attends a DEA ride-along with his brother-in-law Hank. There, he recognizes his former student Jesse Pinkman fleeing a meth lab bust. Walter approaches Jesse with a proposition: use Walter's chemistry expertise and Jesse's street connections to cook and sell methamphetamine.

## Opening Scene

The episode begins with a memorable cold open: a man in his underwear driving an RV through the New Mexico desert, recording a goodbye message to his family. This is Walter White at a crisis point, and the episode then flashes back three weeks to show how he got there.

## Key Plot Points

1. **Cancer Diagnosis**: Walter turns 50 and receives devastating news - inoperable lung cancer with potentially months to live
2. **Financial Desperation**: Realizes his family will be left with nothing after medical bills and funeral costs
3. **DEA Ride-Along**: Accompanies Hank on a meth lab bust, where he spots Jesse Pinkman
4. **The Partnership**: Walter proposes cooking meth with Jesse using Walter's chemistry knowledge
5. **First Cook**: They cook in Jesse's RV in the desert
6. **Confrontation**: Encounter with Krazy-8 and Emilio leads to Walter using chemistry to create deadly gas
7. **Cliffhanger**: Episode ends with Walter waiting for Krazy-8 and Emilio to regain consciousness

## Character Development

### Walter White
- Introduced as mild-mannered, unappreciated chemistry teacher
- Working two jobs (teacher and car wash) to make ends meet
- Passive in his own life, taking disrespect from students and his boss
- The cancer diagnosis acts as catalyst for his transformation
- First glimpse of his ruthlessness when using chemistry to create poison gas

### Jesse Pinkman
- Small-time meth cook and dealer
- Flunked Walter's chemistry class
- Initially cocky but reveals vulnerability
- Surprised by Walter's proposition but sees opportunity

### Skyler White
- Pregnant with second child
- Loving but distracted wife
- Planning Walter's birthday party
- Unaware of Walter's true condition and plans

## Memorable Moments

- Walter's underwear-clad RV drive in the cold open
- "Chemistry is the study of change"
- Walter's awkward handjob birthday "present" from Skyler
- Hank's gun-wielding bravado at the DEA bust
- "You asked how you could help. You asked if you could contribute. Well, here it is - this is how you can help"
- The gas mask cooking scene
- Walter creating phosphine gas to save himself and Jesse

## Themes Introduced

- **Transformation**: The episode title "Pilot" refers both to the series premiere and Walter "piloting" his new life path
- **Desperation**: Financial and medical desperation drive Walter's decision
- **Chemistry as power**: Walter's chemistry knowledge becomes his superpower
- **Pride**: Walter's pride prevents him from accepting charity from his wealthy former colleagues
- **Family**: Walter's stated motivation is providing for his family

## Foreshadowing

- The pants flying in the desert (explained later in the episode)
- Walter's first lie to Skyler about where he was
- The RV that becomes central to their operation
- Krazy-8 who becomes important in upcoming episodes

## Production Notes

- Filmed on location in Albuquerque, New Mexico
- The RV (called "The Crystal Ship") became iconic
- Cold open structure became Breaking Bad trademark
- Vince Gilligan initially pitched the premise as "Mr. Chips becomes Scarface"

## Critical Reception

- Strong reviews lauded Bryan Cranston's dramatic turn
- Established the show's visual style and dark humor
- Set up the premise efficiently while maintaining intrigue
- Won over skeptics who doubted Malcolm in the Middle's dad could play dramatic role

## Episode Connections

- Sets up the entire series premise
- Introduces core characters who appear throughout the show
- Krazy-8 returns in episodes 2-3
- The RV appears throughout seasons 1-3
- Walter's "I am awake" realization marks his transformation beginning

## Quotes

- "Chemistry is, well technically, chemistry is the study of matter. But I prefer to see it as the study of change."
- "What is going on with you lately? You've been so distant. And now this? What, you can't even talk to me any more?"
- "Some straight like you, giant stick up his ass all of a sudden at age what, 60, he's just gonna break bad?"

## Next Episode

Continue with **S01E02 "Cat's in the Bag..."** where Walter and Jesse must deal with the aftermath of their first cook and the two unconscious dealers in Jesse's basement.
```

## Usage Tips

### Uploading to Your Library

1. **Create markdown files** using the templates above
2. **Add appropriate tags** in the content and when uploading
3. **Set category** to `entertainment` when uploading
4. **Vectorize** through standard document upload process

### Tag Strategy

**Movie Tags**: genre, decade, director_name, actor_names, awards, themes
**TV Tags**: tv_show, series_name, genre, network, creator_name, decade, themes
**Episode Tags**: tv_episode, series_name, season_X, episode_descriptor
